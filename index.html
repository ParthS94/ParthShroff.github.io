    Research

    I worked at the Applied Research Laboratories during the Summer of 2019 to develop a software called the Virtual Quantum Optics Laboratory (VQOL) for Quantum-Optics and Quantum Computer Science Simulation

    During the Summer of 2020, I worked with a group of students at the UT Austin Quantum CS FRI to test VQOL by running 10 total quantum experiments. I presented my work at the Northwest Quantum Nexus Workshop in September of 2020.

    Properties of VQOL

    • Models quantum optics as classical optics with real, stochastic vacuum fields
    • Treats measurement outcomes as deterministic threshold-crossing events
    • Post-selects results to only consider valid measurement outcomes
